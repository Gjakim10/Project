PROGRAMME FOR THE 6TH ANNUAL GENERAL MEETING OF THE CONVOCATION OF THE EASTERN AFRICA STATISTICAL TRAINING CENTRE (EASTC)

DATE: THURSDAY, 01ST DECEMBER, 2023

VENUE: FUNCTIONAL HALL

Overview of the 6th Convocation

The 7th convocation of the Eastern Africa Statistical Training Centre of the year 2024 continues the tradition of bringing together academics, industry professionals, alumni, benefactors, and other stakeholders of the university community. It is an opportunity to present and exchange views on topical issues that touch teaching, research, and professional development.

This year's convocation has selected the topic: "the role of official statistics and big data in the economic transformation and sustainable development of Tanzania" as the standing point to reflect on the theme of this year's 18 November African Statistics Day. The theme states "Modernizing data ecosystems to accelerate the implementation of African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA): The role of official statistics and big data in the economic transformation and sustainable development of Africa".

African Statistics Day is celebrated each year on 18th of November. The commemoration aims, among others, to increase public awareness about the important role which statistics play in all aspects of social and economic development. This year's convocation aims to understand the African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA) and the potential role statistics and big data play in discovering markets and serving customers across Africa.

Economic Transformation

In the context of the African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA), reliable statistics are crucial for monitoring trade, identifying market potentials, and promoting intracontinental trade. These data empower decision-makers, help track progress, and respond effectively to crises..

African Statistics Day

In 2023, the theme for African Statistics Day has been chosen to highlight a crucial aspect of our continent's development. This presentation aims to raise awareness among decision-makers, technical and financial partners, data producers and users, researchers, and the public about the vital role of official statistics and big data. By understanding and leveraging these tools, we can accelerate the implementation of the Agreement Establishing the African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA) and significantly promote intracontinental trade.
